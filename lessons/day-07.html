Day 7 Linear Equations & Graphing

Slope & Rate of Change

Vocabulary

Slope
A number measuring the steepness of a line. Calculated as $m = \dfrac{\text{rise}}{\text{run}} = \dfrac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}$.
Rise
The vertical change between two points: $\Delta y = y_2 - y_1$. Positive means up; negative means down.
Run
The horizontal change between two points: $\Delta x = x_2 - x_1$. By convention, move left to right so the run is positive.
Rate of Change
How much one quantity changes per unit change in another. Slope equals the rate of change in real-world contexts (e.g., miles per hour, dollars per item).
Zero Slope
A horizontal line has $m = 0$ because the rise is 0 regardless of how far you travel horizontally.
Undefined Slope
A vertical line has undefined slope because the run is 0, producing division by zero in the slope formula.

Concept

The slope of a line measures how steeply it rises or falls. Given any two points $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$ on the line:

$$m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1} = \frac{\text{rise}}{\text{run}}$$

Rise = 2 (green, vertical); Run = 3 (red, horizontal). Slope $= \dfrac{2}{3}$.

The four types of slope — positive, negative, zero (horizontal), and undefined (vertical).

💡
Rate of change in context: slope is rate of change. If a car travels 180 miles in 3 hours, the slope of its distance-time graph is $m = \dfrac{180}{3} = 60$ miles per hour. Units of slope = (units of $y$) per (unit of $x$).
⚠️
Order matters — but must stay consistent. $\dfrac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}$ and $\dfrac{y_1 - y_2}{x_1 - x_2}$ give the same answer, but mixing (e.g. $\dfrac{y_2 - y_1}{x_1 - x_2}$) flips the sign. Always subtract in the same order top and bottom.

Worked Examples

Example 1 — Slope from Two Points

Find the slope of the line through $(-2, 1)$ and $(4, 13)$.

1
Label: $(x_1, y_1) = (-2, 1)$, $(x_2, y_2) = (4, 13)$.
2
Substitute: $m = \dfrac{13 - 1}{4 - (-2)} = \dfrac{12}{6}$
3
Simplify: $m = 2$
$m = 2$
Example 2 — Negative Slope

Find the slope of the line through $(0, 6)$ and $(4, 2)$.

1
Rise: $y_2 - y_1 = 2 - 6 = -4$ (the line moves down).
2
Run: $x_2 - x_1 = 4 - 0 = 4$
3
$m = \dfrac{-4}{4} = -1$. Negative slope confirms the line falls left to right.
$m = -1$
Example 3 — Rate of Change in Context

A phone plan costs $\$30$ with no calls. After 4 hours of calls it costs $\$54$. Find the rate of change (cost per hour).

1
Write as ordered pairs (hours, dollars): $(0,\, 30)$ and $(4,\, 54)$.
2
$m = \dfrac{54 - 30}{4 - 0} = \dfrac{24}{4} = 6$
3
Units: the rate of change is $\$6$ per hour of calls.
Rate of change = $\$6$ per hour

Practice Problems

Find the slope of the line through $(1, 2)$ and $(4, 8)$.

$m = \dfrac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}$. Subtract $y$-values, then divide by the difference of $x$-values.
$m = \dfrac{8-2}{4-1} = \dfrac{6}{3} = 2$.

Find the slope of the line through $(3, 9)$ and $(6, 3)$.

$\dfrac{3-9}{6-3} = \dfrac{?}{3}$. The rise is negative because the line falls.
$m = \dfrac{3-9}{6-3} = \dfrac{-6}{3} = -2$. Negative slope — line falls left to right.

What is the slope of a horizontal line?

On a horizontal line, two points share the same $y$-value. What is $y_2 - y_1$?
$\Delta y = 0$, so $m = \dfrac{0}{\text{run}} = 0$. Horizontal lines have zero slope.

A line passes through $(5, 3)$ and $(5, 8)$. What is its slope?

Both points have $x = 5$. What is $x_2 - x_1$?
$m = \dfrac{8-3}{5-5} = \dfrac{5}{0}$ — division by zero is undefined. This is a vertical line.

Find the slope of the line through $(-3, 1)$ and $(1, 9)$.

$\dfrac{9-1}{1-(-3)} = \dfrac{8}{?}$. Watch the double negative in the denominator.
$m = \dfrac{9-1}{1-(-3)} = \dfrac{8}{4} = 2$.

A car travels 240 miles in 4 hours at constant speed. What is the rate of change of distance with respect to time?

Rate of change $= \dfrac{\Delta \text{distance}}{\Delta \text{time}} = \dfrac{240}{4}$.
$m = \dfrac{240\text{ mi}}{4\text{ hr}} = 60$ miles per hour.

Which correctly describes a line with slope $m = -3$?

Negative slope → falls left to right. Large $|m|$ → steep.
$m = -3$ is negative (falls left to right) with large absolute value (steep — drops 3 units for every 1 unit right).

Find the slope of the line through $(2, 5)$ and $(6, 5)$.

Both points have the same $y$-value. What is the rise?
$m = \dfrac{5-5}{6-2} = \dfrac{0}{4} = 0$. Horizontal line — zero slope.

Find the slope of the line through $(-2, 4)$ and $(2, -4)$.

$\dfrac{-4-4}{2-(-2)} = \dfrac{-8}{?}$
$m = \dfrac{-4-4}{2-(-2)} = \dfrac{-8}{4} = -2$.

A candle is 12 inches tall when lit. After 3 hours it is 6 inches tall. What is the rate of change of height?

Points: $(0, 12)$ and $(3, 6)$. The candle shrinks, so rate of change is negative.
$m = \dfrac{6-12}{3-0} = \dfrac{-6}{3} = -2$ inches per hour. The candle loses 2 inches each hour.
