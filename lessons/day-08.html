Day 8 Linear Equations & Graphing

Forms of Linear Equations

Vocabulary

Slope-Intercept Form
$y = mx + b$, where $m$ is the slope and $b$ is the $y$-intercept. The most common form for graphing.
Y-Intercept
The point where a line crosses the $y$-axis. Always has $x = 0$, so the point is $(0,\, b)$. Found by setting $x = 0$ in any form.
Point-Slope Form
$y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$, where $m$ is the slope and $(x_1,\, y_1)$ is any known point on the line.
Standard Form
$Ax + By = C$, where $A$, $B$, and $C$ are integers and $A \geq 0$. Useful for finding both intercepts quickly.
X-Intercept
The point where a line crosses the $x$-axis. Always has $y = 0$, so the point is $(x_0,\, 0)$. Found by setting $y = 0$.
Converting Forms
Moving between the three forms using algebra. All three forms describe the same line — they are equivalent equations.

Concept

Every non-vertical line can be written in three equivalent forms. Choose the form that matches the information you're given.

SLOPE-INTERCEPT
$$y = mx + b$$
$m$ = slope  |  $b$ = $y$-intercept
Best for: graphing, reading slope and intercept directly
POINT-SLOPE
$$y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$$
$m$ = slope  |  $(x_1, y_1)$ = known point
Best for: writing an equation from a point + slope (or two points)
STANDARD FORM
$$Ax + By = C$$
$A, B, C$ are integers, $A \geq 0$
Best for: finding both intercepts; common in standardized tests

The three forms are equivalent — you can always convert from one to another using algebra.

🔄
Converting from point-slope to slope-intercept: distribute $m$, then add $y_1$ to both sides.
$y - 3 = 2(x - 1) \;\Rightarrow\; y - 3 = 2x - 2 \;\Rightarrow\; y = 2x + 1$
⚠️
In standard form $Ax + By = C$, the coefficient $A$ must be non-negative and the terms must be integers. If you get $-2x + 3y = 6$, multiply the entire equation by $-1$: $2x - 3y = -6$.

Worked Examples

Example 1 — Write in Slope-Intercept Form

Write the equation of the line with slope $m = -\dfrac{1}{2}$ and $y$-intercept $b = 4$.

1
Recall slope-intercept form: $y = mx + b$.
2
Substitute $m = -\dfrac{1}{2}$ and $b = 4$: $y = -\dfrac{1}{2}x + 4$.
$y = -\dfrac{1}{2}x + 4$
Example 2 — Write Using Point-Slope Form

Write the equation of the line through $(2, 5)$ and $(4, 11)$ in slope-intercept form.

1
Find slope: $m = \dfrac{11-5}{4-2} = \dfrac{6}{2} = 3$
2
Use point-slope with $(x_1, y_1) = (2, 5)$: $y - 5 = 3(x - 2)$
3
Distribute: $y - 5 = 3x - 6 \;\Rightarrow\; y = 3x - 1$
$y = 3x - 1$
Example 3 — Convert to Standard Form

Write $y = \dfrac{3}{4}x - 2$ in standard form.

1
Clear the fraction — multiply both sides by 4: $4y = 3x - 8$
2
Move $x$-term to the left: $-3x + 4y = -8$
3
Make $A$ positive — multiply by $-1$: $3x - 4y = 8$
$3x - 4y = 8$

Practice Problems

What is the $y$-intercept of the line $y = 4x - 7$?

In $y = mx + b$, identify which constant is $b$.
In slope-intercept form $y = 4x - 7$, the $y$-intercept is $b = -7$ (the constant term).

What is the slope of the line $3x - y = 6$?

Solve for $y$ to get slope-intercept form: subtract $3x$, then multiply by $-1$.
$3x - y = 6 \Rightarrow -y = -3x + 6 \Rightarrow y = 3x - 6$. The slope is $m = 3$.

Write the equation of the line with slope $m = -\dfrac{1}{3}$ and $y$-intercept $b = 4$.

Substitute directly: $y = mx + b$ with $m = -\dfrac{1}{3}$ and $b = 4$.
$y = -\dfrac{1}{3}x + 4$. The slope is negative and the $y$-intercept is positive.

What is the $y$-intercept (value of $b$) of the line $y = -5x + 9$?

The equation is already in $y = mx + b$ form. Which number is $b$?
$y = -5x + 9$. The $y$-intercept is $b = 9$ (the constant added at the end).

Write the equation in point-slope form for slope $m = 2$ through the point $(3, 1)$.

Point-slope: $y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$. Here $y_1 = 1$, $m = 2$, $x_1 = 3$.
$y - y_1 = m(x - x_1) \Rightarrow y - 1 = 2(x - 3)$. Option B swaps the point coordinates.

Convert $y - 4 = 3(x + 1)$ to slope-intercept form.

Distribute: $3(x+1) = 3x + 3$. Then add 4 to both sides.
$y - 4 = 3x + 3 \Rightarrow y = 3x + 3 + 4 = 3x + 7$.

Write the equation of the line through $(0, 5)$ with slope $m = -2$.

$(0, 5)$ tells you the $y$-intercept: $b = 5$. Plug in $m$ and $b$.
Since the point is $(0, 5)$, the $y$-intercept is $b = 5$. With $m = -2$: $y = -2x + 5$.

Write $y = \dfrac{2}{3}x - 4$ in standard form.

Multiply both sides by 3 to clear the fraction, then rearrange so $Ax + By = C$ with $A > 0$.
Multiply by 3: $3y = 2x - 12 \Rightarrow -2x + 3y = -12$. Multiply by $-1$: $2x - 3y = 12$.

A line has slope $3$ and passes through $(2, 7)$. Write it in slope-intercept form. What is the $y$-intercept $b$?

Use point-slope: $y - 7 = 3(x - 2)$, then solve for $y$.
$y - 7 = 3(x-2) = 3x - 6 \Rightarrow y = 3x - 6 + 7 = 3x + 1$. So $b = 1$.

What is the slope of the line $3x - 2y = 6$?

Solve for $y$: subtract $3x$, then divide both sides by $-2$.
$-2y = -3x + 6 \Rightarrow y = \dfrac{3}{2}x - 3$. The slope is $m = \dfrac{3}{2}$.
