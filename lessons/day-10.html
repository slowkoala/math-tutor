Day 10 Inequalities

Linear Inequalities

Vocabulary

Inequality
A mathematical statement comparing two expressions using $<$, $>$, $\leq$, or $\geq$. Unlike an equation, it describes a range of solutions.
Solution Set
All values of the variable that make an inequality true. Usually infinite — for example, $x > 3$ is satisfied by 4, 5, 100, 3.001, etc.
Open Circle
A hollow dot on a number line indicating that the boundary value is not included in the solution set. Used for strict inequalities ($<$ or $>$).
Closed Circle
A filled dot on a number line indicating that the boundary value is included. Used for non-strict inequalities ($\leq$ or $\geq$).
Flip the Sign
When multiplying or dividing both sides of an inequality by a negative number, the direction of the inequality symbol reverses. This is the key difference from solving equations.
Compound Inequality
Two inequalities joined by and or or. Example: $-2 \leq x < 5$ means $-2 \leq x$ AND $x < 5$.

Concept

Solving a linear inequality uses the same steps as solving a linear equation, with one critical exception: when you multiply or divide both sides by a negative number, flip the inequality symbol.

Open circle = not included (strict); closed circle = included (non-strict). Row 3 shows a compound "AND" inequality.

⚠️
Flip the sign rule: $-2x > 6$ → divide by $-2$ and flip → $x < -3$.
Adding or subtracting the same value never flips the sign. Only multiplication/division by a negative does.
💡
Checking your answer: pick a value in your solution set and verify it satisfies the original inequality. For $x > 2$, try $x = 5$: substitute back and confirm the inequality is true.

Worked Examples

Example 1 — Basic One-Step Inequality

Solve: $-3x \leq 12$

1
Divide both sides by $-3$. Because we're dividing by a negative, flip the sign: $x \geq \dfrac{12}{-3}$
2
$x \geq -4$. Check with $x = 0$: $-3(0) = 0 \leq 12$ ✓
$x \geq -4$ (closed circle at $-4$, shade right)
Example 2 — Multi-Step Inequality

Solve: $3(x - 1) > 2x + 4$

1
Distribute: $3x - 3 > 2x + 4$
2
Subtract $2x$: $x - 3 > 4$
3
Add 3: $x > 7$. Check with $x = 8$: $3(7) = 21 > 2(8)+4 = 20$ ✓
$x > 7$ (open circle at $7$, shade right)
Example 3 — Real-World Inequality

A concert venue charges $\$12$ admission plus $\$4$ per snack. Mia has $\$40$ to spend. Write and solve an inequality for the number of snacks $s$ she can buy.

1
Total cost: $4s + 12$. Constraint: $4s + 12 \leq 40$
2
Subtract 12: $4s \leq 28$
3
Divide by 4: $s \leq 7$. Mia can buy at most 7 snacks.
$s \leq 7$

Practice Problems

Solve: $2x + 3 > 7$

Subtract 3 from both sides, then divide by 2. No sign flip needed (positive divisor).
$2x > 4 \Rightarrow x > 2$. No sign flip because we divided by positive 2.

Solve: $-3x \leq 12$

Divide by $-3$. Since you're dividing by a negative, flip the inequality symbol.
$\dfrac{-3x}{-3} \geq \dfrac{12}{-3} \Rightarrow x \geq -4$. Sign flipped because of division by $-3$.

Solve: $5 - 2x < 11$

Subtract 5: $-2x < 6$. Then divide by $-2$ — don't forget to flip!
$-2x < 6 \Rightarrow x > -3$ (flipped when dividing by $-2$).

Solve $4x - 1 \geq 11$. What is the boundary value (the number $x$ is compared to)?

Add 1, then divide by 4. No negative divisor, so no sign flip.
$4x \geq 12 \Rightarrow x \geq 3$. The boundary is $3$ (closed circle, since $\geq$).

Which number line graph correctly shows $x < 4$?

$x < 4$ means all values less than (to the left of) 4. The strict $<$ means 4 is NOT included.
Strict inequality $<$ → open circle. "Less than" → arrow points left (toward smaller numbers).

Solve: $3(x - 2) > 2x + 1$

Distribute the left side first: $3x - 6 > 2x + 1$. Then isolate $x$.
$3x - 6 > 2x + 1 \Rightarrow x - 6 > 1 \Rightarrow x > 7$.

An inequality has solution $x \geq -2$. Which value is in the solution set?

The solution includes all values greater than or equal to $-2$. Which option is $\geq -2$?
$5 \geq -2$ ✓. All other choices are less than $-2$, so they fail the inequality.

A number line shows a closed circle at $1$ with an arrow pointing right. Which inequality does this represent?

Closed circle = "or equal to." Arrow right = "greater than."
Closed circle → $\leq$ or $\geq$. Arrow right → greater than. Together: $x \geq 1$.

Solve $2(x + 3) \geq x + 10$. What is the boundary value?

Distribute: $2x + 6 \geq x + 10$. Then subtract $x$ and 6.
$2x + 6 \geq x + 10 \Rightarrow x \geq 4$. Boundary is $4$.

A test requires at least 70 points to pass. Which inequality represents the score $s$ needed to pass?

"At least 70" means 70 is the minimum — 70 itself counts. Which symbol includes the boundary?
"At least 70" → $s \geq 70$. A score of exactly 70 passes, and any score above 70 also passes.
