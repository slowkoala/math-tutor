Day 13 Systems of Equations

Systems of Equations: Elimination

Vocabulary

Elimination Method
Adding or subtracting two equations so that one variable cancels out. The remaining single-variable equation can then be solved directly.
Opposite Coefficients
When the coefficients of the same variable in two equations are additive inverses (e.g., $+3y$ and $-3y$), adding the equations eliminates that variable.
Multiplier
A constant used to scale one (or both) equations so that a variable's coefficients become opposites. Essential when coefficients don't already match.
Equivalent System
A system with the same solution set as the original. Multiplying an equation by a non-zero constant produces an equivalent system.
Back-Substitution
After finding one variable's value, substituting it back into any equation from the original system to find the other variable.
Linear Combination
Adding multiples of two equations together. Elimination is a specific use of linear combinations to remove a variable.

Concept

The elimination method adds the two equations together (or subtracts one from the other) so that one variable disappears. This leaves a simple one-variable equation to solve.

The $+y$ and $-y$ terms cancel when the equations are added, leaving a single equation in $x$.

🔢
When coefficients don't already cancel: multiply one equation (or both) by a constant first. To eliminate $y$ from $3x + 2y = 11$ and $x + y = 5$: multiply the second by $-2$ to get $-2x - 2y = -10$, then add.
⚠️
If after adding/subtracting both variables cancel and you get $0 = 0$ → infinite solutions (same line). If you get $0 = k$ where $k \neq 0$ → no solution (parallel lines).

Worked Examples

Example 1 — Add to Eliminate

Solve: $x + y = 8$ and $x - y = 2$

1
The $y$ terms are $+y$ and $-y$ (opposites). Add the equations:
2
$(x+y)+(x-y)=8+2 \Rightarrow 2x = 10 \Rightarrow x = 5$
3
Back-substitute: $5 + y = 8 \Rightarrow y = 3$.
4
Check: $5-3 = 2$ ✓
$(5, 3)$
Example 2 — Multiply First, Then Eliminate

Solve: $2x + 3y = 11$ and $x + 2y = 7$

1
Multiply the second equation by $-2$: $-2x - 4y = -14$.
2
Add to the first: $(2x+3y)+(-2x-4y) = 11+(-14) \Rightarrow -y = -3 \Rightarrow y = 3$.
3
Back-substitute: $x + 2(3) = 7 \Rightarrow x = 1$.
4
Check: $2(1)+3(3)=11$ ✓
$(1, 3)$
Example 3 — Multiply Both Equations

Solve: $4x + 3y = 24$ and $2x - y = 2$

1
Multiply the second by 3: $6x - 3y = 6$.
2
Add: $4x+3y+6x-3y = 24+6 \Rightarrow 10x = 30 \Rightarrow x = 3$.
3
Back-substitute: $2(3)-y=2 \Rightarrow y=4$.
$(3, 4)$

Practice Problems

Solve by elimination: $x + y = 8$ and $x - y = 2$.

Add the equations: the $y$ terms cancel. Solve for $x$, then back-substitute.
Adding: $2x=10 \Rightarrow x=5$. Then $5+y=8 \Rightarrow y=3$.

Solve: $2x + y = 7$ and $x + y = 4$.

Subtract the second from the first to eliminate $y$.
$(2x+y)-(x+y)=7-4 \Rightarrow x=3$. Then $3+y=4 \Rightarrow y=1$.

Solve $x + 2y = 7$ and $x - 2y = 1$ by elimination. What is $x$?

Add the two equations. The $2y$ and $-2y$ terms cancel.
Adding: $2x = 8 \Rightarrow x = 4$. (Then $4+2y=7 \Rightarrow y=1.5$.)

Solve: $2x + y = 9$ and $4x - y = 3$.

$+y$ and $-y$ are opposites. Add the equations to eliminate $y$.
Adding: $6x=12 \Rightarrow x=2$. Then $4+y=9 \Rightarrow y=5$. Solution: $(2,5)$.

Solve: $2x + 3y = 11$ and $x + 2y = 7$.

Multiply the second equation by $-2$, then add to the first to eliminate $x$.
$-2(x+2y=7) \Rightarrow -2x-4y=-14$. Add: $-y=-3 \Rightarrow y=3$. Then $x+6=7 \Rightarrow x=1$.

Solve $3x + y = 11$ and $x + y = 5$ by elimination. What is $y$?

Subtract the second from the first: $3x+y-(x+y)=11-5$. This eliminates $y$. Find $x$, then back-sub.
$2x=6 \Rightarrow x=3$. Then $3+y=5 \Rightarrow y=2$.

Solve: $2x + y = 10$ and $2x + 3y = 14$.

Subtract the first from the second to eliminate $2x$.
$(2x+3y)-(2x+y)=14-10 \Rightarrow 2y=4 \Rightarrow y=2$. Then $2x+2=10 \Rightarrow x=4$.

After eliminating one variable, you get the equation $0 = 0$. What does this mean about the system?

$0=0$ is always true — it doesn't restrict $x$ or $y$ at all.
$0=0$ is a tautology → infinite solutions (dependent system, same line).

Solve: $3x - 2y = 4$ and $6x - 4y = 8$.

Multiply the first equation by 2. Compare the result to the second equation.
$2 \times (3x-2y=4) = 6x-4y=8$, which is identical to equation 2. The equations are the same line → infinite solutions.

Solve: $4x + 3y = 24$ and $2x - y = 2$.

Multiply the second equation by 3 to make the $y$-coefficients opposites ($+3y$ and $-3y$).
$3(2x-y=2) \Rightarrow 6x-3y=6$. Add: $10x=30 \Rightarrow x=3$. Then $2(3)-y=2 \Rightarrow y=4$.
