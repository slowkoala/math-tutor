Day 17 Functions

Exponential Growth & Decay

Vocabulary

Exponential Function
A function of the form $f(x) = a \cdot b^x$ where $b > 0$ and $b \ne 1$.
Growth Factor
The value $b > 1$ in $f(x) = a \cdot b^x$; the output multiplies by $b$ each step.
Decay Factor
The value $0 < b < 1$ in $f(x) = a \cdot b^x$; the output shrinks by factor $b$ each step.
Initial Value ($a$)
The value of the function when $x = 0$: $f(0) = a \cdot b^0 = a$.
Percent Rate
If growing at $r\%$ per period, $b = 1 + \tfrac{r}{100}$. If decaying, $b = 1 - \tfrac{r}{100}$.
Asymptote
A line the graph approaches but never touches. Exponential functions approach $y = 0$ as $x \to -\infty$ (growth) or $x \to +\infty$ (decay).

Concept

Exponential Growth
$f(x) = a \cdot b^x,\quad b > 1$
Example: $f(x) = 100 \cdot 2^x$
Doubles every step
Exponential Decay
$f(x) = a \cdot b^x,\quad 0 < b < 1$
Example: $f(x) = 100 \cdot (0.5)^x$
Halves every step

Growth curves rise steeply; decay curves fall and approach the x-axis asymptotically.

💡
Identify growth vs. decay: If $b > 1$ → growth. If $0 < b < 1$ → decay. The initial value $a = f(0)$ is the y-intercept.

Worked Examples

Example 1 — Model exponential growth and evaluate

A bacteria culture starts with 500 cells and doubles every hour. Write a function and find the count after 3 hours.

1
Model: $f(t) = 500 \cdot 2^t$, where $t$ = hours.
2
Identify: $a = 500$ (initial), $b = 2$ (doubles → growth).
3
After 3 hours: $f(3) = 500 \cdot 2^3 = 500 \cdot 8 = 4000$ cells.
$f(t) = 500 \cdot 2^t$; after 3 hours: 4000 cells.
Example 2 — Model exponential decay (depreciation)

A car worth $20,000 depreciates at 15% per year. Write a model and find its value after 4 years.

1
Decay factor: $b = 1 - 0.15 = 0.85$.
2
Model: $V(t) = 20000 \cdot (0.85)^t$
3
After 4 years: $V(4) = 20000 \cdot (0.85)^4$
4
$(0.85)^4 \approx 0.522$, so $V(4) \approx 20000 \cdot 0.522 = \$10{,}440$.
$V(t) = 20000 \cdot (0.85)^t$; after 4 years: approximately $\$10{,}440$.
Example 3 — Identify growth or decay from an equation

Given $f(x) = 3 \cdot (0.25)^x$, identify the initial value and state whether it is growth or decay.

1
Initial value: $a = 3$ (the coefficient).
2
Base: $b = 0.25$. Since $0 < 0.25 < 1$, this is decay.
3
Verify: $f(0) = 3 \cdot 1 = 3$, $f(1) = 3 \cdot 0.25 = 0.75$ — decreasing. ✓
Initial value: 3. Exponential decay (base $0.25 < 1$).

Practice Problems

$f(x) = 4 \cdot 3^x$ — is this growth or decay?

Look at the base $b$ in $f(x) = a \cdot b^x$. Is it greater than 1 or between 0 and 1?
The base $b = 3 > 1$, so this is exponential growth. The value of $a$ (initial value) does not determine growth vs. decay.

What is the initial value of $f(x) = 200 \cdot (0.8)^x$?

The initial value is $f(0) = a \cdot b^0 = a \cdot 1 = a$.
$f(0) = 200 \cdot (0.8)^0 = 200 \cdot 1 = 200$. The initial value is the coefficient $a = 200$.

If $f(x) = 50 \cdot 2^x$, find $f(3)$.

$f(3) = 50 \cdot 2^3$. What is $2^3$?
$f(3) = 50 \cdot 2^3 = 50 \cdot 8 = 400$.

A town's population starts at 1,000 and grows 20% per year. Which model is correct?

A 20% growth rate means the population is multiplied by $1 + 0.20 = 1.20$ each year.
Growing 20% per year means the growth factor is $b = 1.20$. With initial value 1000, the model is $P(t) = 1000 \cdot (1.20)^t$.

If $f(x) = 800 \cdot (0.5)^x$, find $f(4)$.

$(0.5)^4 = 0.5 \times 0.5 \times 0.5 \times 0.5$. Halve the value four times starting from 800.
$f(4) = 800 \cdot (0.5)^4 = 800 \cdot 0.0625 = 50$. Each step halves: $800 \to 400 \to 200 \to 100 \to 50$.

Does $f(x) = 0.1 \cdot (1.5)^x$ represent growth or decay?

Only the base $b$ determines growth vs. decay, not the initial value $a$.
Base $b = 1.5 > 1$ → exponential growth. The initial value $a = 0.1$ only tells us where the function starts; it doesn't affect whether it grows or decays.

A car worth $30,000 depreciates 10% per year. What is its value (in dollars) after 1 year?

The decay factor is $1 - 0.10 = 0.90$. Multiply the initial value by this factor once.
$V(1) = 30000 \cdot (0.90)^1 = 30000 \cdot 0.90 = 27000$. The car loses $3,000 in value in the first year.

Which equation represents exponential decay?

Decay requires $0 < b < 1$. Which option has a base between 0 and 1?
Option A: $b=3>1$ (growth). Option B: $b=0.6$, and $0 < 0.6 < 1$ → decay. Option C is linear. Option D: $b=1.1>1$ (growth).

If $f(x) = 9 \cdot \left(\tfrac{1}{3}\right)^x$, find $f(2)$.

$\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^2 = \frac{1}{9}$. Multiply $9 \cdot \frac{1}{9}$.
$f(2) = 9 \cdot \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^2 = 9 \cdot \frac{1}{9} = 1$.

As $x \to +\infty$, the function $f(x) = 5 \cdot (0.4)^x$ approaches which value?

This is decay ($b = 0.4 < 1$). What happens to $(0.4)^x$ as $x$ gets very large?
As $x \to +\infty$, $(0.4)^x \to 0$, so $f(x) = 5 \cdot (0.4)^x \to 0$. The x-axis ($y = 0$) is the horizontal asymptote for this decay function.
