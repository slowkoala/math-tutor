Day 19 Polynomials & Quadratics

Factoring Quadratics

Vocabulary

Quadratic Expression
A polynomial of degree 2: $ax^2 + bx + c$ where $a \ne 0$.
Factoring
Rewriting a polynomial as a product of simpler polynomials. The reverse of expanding.
Greatest Common Factor (GCF)
The largest factor that divides all terms; always factor out GCF first.
Zero Product Property
If $A \cdot B = 0$, then $A = 0$ or $B = 0$. Used to solve factored equations.
Zeros / Roots
The x-values where a quadratic equals zero; the x-intercepts of the parabola.
ac Method
For $ax^2+bx+c$: find two numbers that multiply to $ac$ and add to $b$, then factor by grouping.

Concept

💡
Flowchart — always follow this order:
  1. GCF first — factor out the greatest common factor.
  2. Check the number of terms:
    • 2 terms → difference of squares? $a^2 - b^2 = (a+b)(a-b)$
    • 3 terms, $a=1$ → find two numbers multiplying to $c$ and adding to $b$.
    • 3 terms, $a \ne 1$ → use the ac method.
  3. Check your answer by expanding (FOIL).

The box method shows each product; combine the middle terms to verify $3x + 4x = 7x$.

Worked Examples

Example 1 — Factor a monic trinomial ($a = 1$)

Factor: $x^2 + 5x + 6$

1
GCF: None (no common factor).
2
Find two numbers: multiply to 6, add to 5.
Pairs: $1 \times 6 = 6$, $1+6=7$; $2 \times 3=6$, $2+3=5$ ✓
3
Factor: $(x+2)(x+3)$
4
Check: $(x+2)(x+3) = x^2+3x+2x+6 = x^2+5x+6$ ✓
$x^2 + 5x + 6 = (x+2)(x+3)$
Example 2 — Factor with the ac method ($a \ne 1$)

Factor: $2x^2 + 7x + 3$

1
GCF: None.
2
ac method: $a \cdot c = 2 \cdot 3 = 6$. Need two numbers multiplying to 6, adding to 7: $1$ and $6$.
3
Rewrite: $2x^2 + x + 6x + 3$
4
Group: $x(2x+1) + 3(2x+1) = (x+3)(2x+1)$
$2x^2 + 7x + 3 = (x+3)(2x+1)$
Example 3 — Solve a quadratic by factoring

Solve: $x^2 - x - 12 = 0$

1
Factor: Find two numbers multiplying to $-12$, adding to $-1$: $-4$ and $3$.
$(x-4)(x+3) = 0$
2
Zero Product: $x-4=0$ or $x+3=0$
3
$x = 4$ or $x = -3$
$x = 4$ or $x = -3$.

Practice Problems

Factor: $x^2 + 8x + 15$

Find two numbers that multiply to 15 and add to 8.
$3 \times 5 = 15$ and $3 + 5 = 8$. So $x^2+8x+15 = (x+3)(x+5)$. Check: $(x+3)(x+5) = x^2+8x+15$ ✓.

Factor: $x^2 - 9x + 18$

Find two negative numbers that multiply to 18 and add to $-9$.
$(-3) \times (-6) = 18$ and $(-3) + (-6) = -9$. So $x^2-9x+18 = (x-3)(x-6)$.

Factor: $x^2 + 3x - 10$

Find two numbers multiplying to $-10$ and adding to $+3$. One must be positive, one negative.
$5 \times (-2) = -10$ and $5 + (-2) = 3$. So $x^2+3x-10 = (x+5)(x-2)$.

Factor out the GCF from $3x^2 + 9x$.

The GCF of $3x^2$ and $9x$ includes both the common number factor and the common variable factor.
GCF of $3x^2$ and $9x$ is $3x$. Factoring: $3x^2 + 9x = 3x(x + 3)$. Verify: $3x \cdot x = 3x^2$ ✓ and $3x \cdot 3 = 9x$ ✓.

Solve $x^2 + 6x + 8 = 0$. What is the larger root?

Factor: find two numbers multiplying to 8, adding to 6. Then use the Zero Product Property.
$x^2+6x+8 = (x+2)(x+4) = 0$. Roots: $x = -2$ or $x = -4$. The larger root is $-2$.

Solve $x^2 - 5x + 6 = 0$. What is the smaller root?

Find two numbers multiplying to 6 and adding to $-5$. Both should be negative.
$x^2-5x+6 = (x-2)(x-3) = 0$. Roots: $x = 2$ or $x = 3$. The smaller root is $2$.

Factor: $x^2 - 16$

Recognize the difference of squares pattern: $a^2 - b^2 = (a+b)(a-b)$. Here $a = x$ and $b = 4$.
$x^2 - 16 = x^2 - 4^2 = (x+4)(x-4)$. This is the difference of squares pattern.

Factor: $2x^2 + 5x - 3$

Use the ac method: $ac = 2 \times (-3) = -6$. Find two numbers multiplying to $-6$ and adding to $5$.
$ac = -6$; need numbers multiplying to $-6$ adding to $5$: $6$ and $-1$. Rewrite: $2x^2+6x-x-3 = 2x(x+3)-1(x+3) = (2x-1)(x+3)$.

Solve $x^2 - 2x - 15 = 0$. What is the sum of the two roots?

Factor first: find two numbers multiplying to $-15$ and adding to $-2$. Then find both roots.
$x^2-2x-15 = (x-5)(x+3) = 0$. Roots: $x=5$ or $x=-3$. Sum: $5 + (-3) = 2$.

Factor: $4x^2 - 25$

Recognize the difference of squares: $4x^2 = (2x)^2$ and $25 = 5^2$.
$4x^2 - 25 = (2x)^2 - 5^2 = (2x+5)(2x-5)$. Check: $(2x+5)(2x-5) = 4x^2 - 10x + 10x - 25 = 4x^2 - 25$ ✓.
