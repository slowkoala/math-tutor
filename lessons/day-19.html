Day 19 Polynomials & Quadratics

Factoring Quadratics

Key Vocabulary

Quadratic Expression
A polynomial of degree 2: \(ax^2 + bx + c\) where \(a \ne 0\).
Factoring
Rewriting a polynomial as a product of simpler polynomials. The reverse of expanding.
Greatest Common Factor (GCF)
The largest factor that divides all terms; always factor out GCF first.
Zero Product Property
If \(A \cdot B = 0\), then \(A = 0\) or \(B = 0\). Used to solve factored equations.
Zeros / Roots
The x-values where a quadratic equals zero; the x-intercepts of the parabola.
ac Method
For \(ax^2+bx+c\): find two numbers that multiply to \(ac\) and add to \(b\), then factor by grouping.

Factoring Strategy

Flowchart — always follow this order:
  1. GCF first — factor out the greatest common factor.
  2. Check the number of terms:
    • 2 terms → difference of squares? \(a^2 - b^2 = (a+b)(a-b)\)
    • 3 terms, \(a=1\) → find two numbers multiplying to \(c\) and adding to \(b\).
    • 3 terms, \(a \ne 1\) → use the ac method.
  3. Check your answer by expanding (FOIL).

Worked Examples

Factor: \(x^2 + 5x + 6\)

Step 1 — GCF: None (no common factor).

Step 2 — Find two numbers: multiply to 6, add to 5.

Pairs: \(1 \times 6 = 6\), \(1+6=7\); \(2 \times 3=6\), \(2+3=5\) ✓

Step 3 — Factor: \((x+2)(x+3)\)

Check: \((x+2)(x+3) = x^2+3x+2x+6 = x^2+5x+6\) ✓

Factor: \(2x^2 + 7x + 3\)

Step 1 — GCF: None.

Step 2 — ac method: \(a \cdot c = 2 \cdot 3 = 6\). Need two numbers multiplying to 6, adding to 7: \(1\) and \(6\).

Step 3 — Rewrite: \(2x^2 + x + 6x + 3\)

Step 4 — Group: \(x(2x+1) + 3(2x+1) = (x+3)(2x+1)\)

Solve: \(x^2 - x - 12 = 0\)

Step 1 — Factor: Find two numbers multiplying to \(-12\), adding to \(-1\): \(-4\) and \(3\).

\((x-4)(x+3) = 0\)

Step 2 — Zero Product: \(x-4=0\) or \(x+3=0\)

\(x = 4\) or \(x = -3\)

Practice Problems

Factor: \(x^2 + 8x + 15\)
  1. \((x+1)(x+15)\)
  2. \((x+4)(x+4)\)
  3. \((x+3)(x+5)\)
  4. \((x+6)(x+2)\)
Factor: \(x^2 - 9x + 18\)
  1. \((x-2)(x-7)\)
  2. \((x+3)(x-6)\)
  3. \((x-9)(x+2)\)
  4. \((x-3)(x-6)\)
Factor: \(x^2 + 3x - 10\)
  1. \((x-5)(x+2)\)
  2. \((x+5)(x-2)\)
  3. \((x+10)(x-1)\)
  4. \((x-10)(x+1)\)
Factor out the GCF from \(3x^2 + 9x\).
  1. \(3x(x + 3)\)
  2. \(3(x^2 + 9x)\)
  3. \(x(3x + 9)\)
  4. \(9x(x + 1)\)
Solve \(x^2 + 6x + 8 = 0\). What is the larger root?
Solve \(x^2 - 5x + 6 = 0\). What is the smaller root?
Factor: \(x^2 - 16\)
  1. \((x-4)^2\)
  2. \((x+8)(x-8)\)
  3. \((x+4)(x-4)\)
  4. \((x-2)(x-8)\)
Factor: \(2x^2 + 5x - 3\)
  1. \((2x+3)(x-1)\)
  2. \((x+3)(2x+1)\)
  3. \((2x-3)(x+1)\)
  4. \((2x-1)(x+3)\)
Solve \(x^2 - 2x - 15 = 0\). What is the sum of the two roots?
Factor: \(4x^2 - 25\)
  1. \((2x+5)(2x-5)\)
  2. \((4x+5)(x-5)\)
  3. \((2x-5)^2\)
  4. \((4x-25)(x+1)\)
