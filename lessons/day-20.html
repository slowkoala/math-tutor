Day 20 Polynomials & Quadratics

Polynomials: Operations & Special Products

Vocabulary

Polynomial
A sum of terms of the form $ax^n$ where $n$ is a non-negative integer. Examples: $3x^2 - 2x + 1$.
Degree
The highest exponent in a polynomial. $3x^4 - x + 7$ has degree 4.
Leading Coefficient
The coefficient of the term with the highest degree.
FOIL
First, Outer, Inner, Last — a method for multiplying two binomials.
Perfect Square Trinomial
$(a+b)^2 = a^2+2ab+b^2$ and $(a-b)^2 = a^2-2ab+b^2$.
Difference of Squares
$a^2 - b^2 = (a+b)(a-b)$. Only works with subtraction, not addition.

Concept

Difference of Squares
$a^2 - b^2 = (a+b)(a-b)$
Example: $x^2 - 9 = (x+3)(x-3)$
Perfect Square (Sum)
$(a+b)^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2$
Example: $(x+4)^2 = x^2+8x+16$
Perfect Square (Diff)
$(a-b)^2 = a^2 - 2ab + b^2$
Example: $(x-5)^2 = x^2-10x+25$

FOIL connects each term in the first binomial to each term in the second.

Worked Examples

Example 1 — Add polynomials by combining like terms

Add: $(3x^2 - 2x + 5) + (x^2 + 4x - 3)$

1
Group like terms:
2
$x^2$ terms: $3x^2 + x^2 = 4x^2$
3
$x$ terms: $-2x + 4x = 2x$
4
Constants: $5 + (-3) = 2$
$4x^2 + 2x + 2$
Example 2 — Expand a perfect square binomial

Multiply: $(x+5)^2$

1
Use the perfect square formula: $(a+b)^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2$
2
Here $a = x$ and $b = 5$: $x^2 + 2(x)(5) + 5^2 = x^2 + 10x + 25$
3
Warning: $(x+5)^2 \ne x^2 + 25$! Don't forget the middle term $2ab$.
$(x+5)^2 = x^2 + 10x + 25$
Example 3 — Multiply a difference of squares pattern

Multiply: $(3x-2)(3x+2)$

1
Recognize the pattern: $(a-b)(a+b) = a^2 - b^2$
2
Here $a = 3x$ and $b = 2$: $(3x)^2 - 2^2 = 9x^2 - 4$
$(3x-2)(3x+2) = 9x^2 - 4$

Practice Problems

Add: $(x^2 + 3x - 1) + (2x^2 - x + 4)$

Combine like terms: add the $x^2$ terms, the $x$ terms, and the constants separately.
$x^2+2x^2=3x^2$; $3x+(-x)=2x$; $(-1)+4=3$. Answer: $3x^2+2x+3$.

Subtract: $(4x^2 - 5x + 2) - (x^2 + 3x - 1)$

Distribute the negative sign: $-(x^2+3x-1) = -x^2-3x+1$, then add.
$(4x^2-5x+2)+(-x^2-3x+1) = 3x^2-8x+3$.

Multiply: $(x+3)(x-5)$

Use FOIL: F = $x \cdot x$, O = $x \cdot (-5)$, I = $3 \cdot x$, L = $3 \cdot (-5)$.
$(x+3)(x-5) = x^2 - 5x + 3x - 15 = x^2 - 2x - 15$.

Expand $(x-7)^2$. What is the coefficient of the $x$ term?

Use $(a-b)^2 = a^2 - 2ab + b^2$ with $a=x$ and $b=7$. The middle term is $-2ab$.
$(x-7)^2 = x^2 - 2(x)(7) + 49 = x^2 - 14x + 49$. The coefficient of $x$ is $-14$.

Multiply: $(2x+1)(3x-4)$

FOIL: $2x \cdot 3x = 6x^2$; $2x \cdot (-4) = -8x$; $1 \cdot 3x = 3x$; $1 \cdot (-4) = -4$. Combine middle terms.
$(2x+1)(3x-4) = 6x^2 - 8x + 3x - 4 = 6x^2 - 5x - 4$.

Expand $(x+6)^2$. What is the constant term?

Use $(a+b)^2 = a^2+2ab+b^2$. The constant term is $b^2$ where $b=6$.
$(x+6)^2 = x^2 + 12x + 36$. The constant term is $6^2 = 36$.

Multiply: $(5x-1)(5x+1)$

Recognize the difference of squares pattern: $(a-b)(a+b) = a^2 - b^2$ with $a=5x$ and $b=1$.
$(5x-1)(5x+1) = (5x)^2 - 1^2 = 25x^2 - 1$. The middle terms cancel in the difference of squares pattern.

What is the degree of the product of a degree-2 polynomial and a degree-3 polynomial?

When multiplying polynomials, the highest-degree term comes from multiplying the leading terms. What's $x^2 \cdot x^3$?
The leading term of the product is $x^2 \cdot x^3 = x^5$, so the degree is $2 + 3 = 5$.

What is the leading coefficient of $(3x^2 + x)(2x - 5)$?

The leading coefficient comes from multiplying the leading coefficients of each factor: $3 \times 2$.
The highest-degree term is $3x^2 \cdot 2x = 6x^3$. So the leading coefficient is $6$.

Expand $(x+2)(x-2)(x+3)$. What is the constant term?

First multiply $(x+2)(x-2) = x^2 - 4$ using difference of squares. Then multiply by $(x+3)$.
$(x+2)(x-2) = x^2-4$. Then $(x^2-4)(x+3) = x^3+3x^2-4x-12$. The constant term is $(-4)(3) = -12$.
