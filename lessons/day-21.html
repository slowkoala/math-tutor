Day 21 Polynomials & Quadratics

Graphing Quadratic Functions

Vocabulary

Parabola
The U-shaped graph of a quadratic function $f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c$.
Vertex
The turning point of the parabola — the minimum if $a>0$, maximum if $a<0$.
Axis of Symmetry
The vertical line through the vertex: $x = -\dfrac{b}{2a}$.
Vertex Form
$f(x) = a(x-h)^2 + k$ — vertex is at $(h, k)$.
Opens Up / Down
If $a > 0$, parabola opens up (min vertex). If $a < 0$, opens down (max vertex).
x-intercepts
Points where the parabola crosses the x-axis; the zeros/roots of the quadratic.

Concept

For $f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c$:

  • Vertex x-coordinate: $x = -\dfrac{b}{2a}$
  • Vertex y-coordinate: substitute x back into f(x)
  • y-intercept: $f(0) = c$
  • x-intercepts: factor or use quadratic formula

$f(x) = x^2 - 4x + 3$: vertex at $(2,-1)$, x-intercepts at 1 and 3, y-intercept at 3.

Worked Examples

Example 1 — Find the vertex and axis of symmetry

Find the vertex and axis of symmetry of $f(x) = x^2 - 6x + 8$.

1
Axis of symmetry: $x = -\dfrac{b}{2a} = -\dfrac{-6}{2(1)} = 3$
2
Vertex y: $f(3) = 9 - 18 + 8 = -1$
3
Vertex: $(3, -1)$. Axis of symmetry: $x = 3$.
Vertex $(3,-1)$; axis of symmetry $x = 3$.
Example 2 — Convert to vertex form by completing the square

Write $f(x) = x^2 - 4x + 7$ in vertex form.

1
Complete the square: $f(x) = (x^2 - 4x \phantom{+4}) + 7$
2
Add and subtract $\left(\tfrac{4}{2}\right)^2 = 4$: $f(x) = (x^2 - 4x + 4) + 7 - 4$
3
$f(x) = (x-2)^2 + 3$. Vertex: $(2, 3)$.
$f(x) = (x-2)^2 + 3$; vertex at $(2,3)$.
Example 3 — Find the maximum height of a projectile

A ball is thrown upward. Its height in feet is $h(t) = -16t^2 + 64t + 5$. What is the maximum height?

1
The maximum occurs at the vertex (since $a = -16 < 0$).
2
t at vertex: $t = -\dfrac{64}{2(-16)} = -\dfrac{64}{-32} = 2$ seconds.
3
Max height: $h(2) = -16(4) + 64(2) + 5 = -64 + 128 + 5 = 69$ feet.
Maximum height: 69 feet, reached at $t = 2$ seconds.

Practice Problems

Find the axis of symmetry of $f(x) = x^2 - 8x + 12$.

Use the formula $x = -\dfrac{b}{2a}$ with $a=1$ and $b=-8$.
$x = -\dfrac{-8}{2(1)} = \dfrac{8}{2} = 4$. The axis of symmetry is $x = 4$.

Using $f(x) = x^2 - 8x + 12$ and axis of symmetry $x = 4$, find the y-coordinate of the vertex.

Substitute $x = 4$ into $f(x) = x^2 - 8x + 12$.
$f(4) = 16 - 32 + 12 = -4$. The vertex is $(4, -4)$.

Does $f(x) = 2x^2 + 4x - 6$ open up or down?

The sign of the leading coefficient $a$ determines whether the parabola opens up or down.
$a = 2 > 0$, so the parabola opens upward and has a minimum vertex. The values of $b$ and $c$ do not determine orientation.

What is the y-intercept of $f(x) = 3x^2 - 2x + 7$?

The y-intercept is $f(0)$. In $ax^2+bx+c$, this is just the constant $c$.
$f(0) = 3(0)^2 - 2(0) + 7 = 7$. The y-intercept equals the constant term $c = 7$.

What is the x-coordinate of the vertex of $f(x) = (x-3)^2 + 5$?

In vertex form $f(x) = a(x-h)^2 + k$, the vertex is at $(h,k)$. What is $h$ here?
The function is in vertex form $(x-3)^2 + 5$, so $h=3$ and $k=5$. Vertex: $(3,5)$. x-coordinate = 3.

Does $f(x) = -(x-1)^2 + 9$ have a maximum or minimum value?

The negative sign in front means $a = -1 < 0$, so the parabola opens downward. The vertex gives the highest point.
$a = -1 < 0$ → parabola opens down → vertex is a maximum. Vertex is at $(1,9)$, so the maximum value is $9$.

The x-intercepts of $f(x) = x^2 - 5x + 4$ are $x = 1$ and $x = 4$. What is their product?

Simply multiply the two roots: $1 \times 4$.
Product of roots $= 1 \times 4 = 4$. (Note: for $x^2 + bx + c$, the product of roots always equals $c$. Here $c=4$ ✓.)

Find the axis of symmetry of $f(x) = -3x^2 + 12x - 1$.

Use $x = -\dfrac{b}{2a}$ with $a = -3$ and $b = 12$.
$x = -\dfrac{12}{2(-3)} = -\dfrac{12}{-6} = 2$. Axis of symmetry: $x = 2$.

What is the minimum value of $f(x) = (x+2)^2 - 3$?

In vertex form $a(x-h)^2+k$, the vertex is at $(h,k)$. What is $k$? Since $a > 0$, it's a minimum.
Vertex form $(x+2)^2 - 3$ has vertex $(-2,-3)$. Since $a=1>0$, the parabola opens up and the minimum value is $k = -3$.

A ball's height is $h(t) = -16t^2 + 32t + 5$. What is the maximum height in feet?

Find the vertex: $t = -\dfrac{b}{2a} = -\dfrac{32}{2(-16)}$. Then evaluate $h$ at that time.
$t = -\dfrac{32}{-32} = 1$. $h(1) = -16(1) + 32(1) + 5 = -16 + 32 + 5 = 21$ feet.
