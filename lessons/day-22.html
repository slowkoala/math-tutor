Day 22 Data & Statistics

Bivariate Data & Linear Regression

Vocabulary

Scatter Plot
A graph of ordered pairs showing the relationship between two numerical variables.
Correlation
The direction and strength of the linear relationship between two variables. Positive, negative, or none.
Line of Best Fit
A line that best represents the trend in a scatter plot; used to make predictions.
Linear Regression
A method to find the equation of the line of best fit for bivariate data.
Residual
The difference between an actual data value and the predicted value from the line of best fit.
Interpolation / Extrapolation
Interpolation predicts within the data range; extrapolation predicts beyond it (less reliable).

Concept

A scatter plot displays bivariate data — each point is an ordered pair $(x, y)$. The shape of the cloud of points tells us whether there is a correlation between the two variables.

Correlation types: positive (left), negative (center), none (right).

💡
Using the line of best fit: Once you have the equation $\hat{y} = mx + b$, plug in any x to predict y. The slope tells you the rate — how much y changes per unit of x.

Worked Examples

Example 1 — Predict from a line of best fit

A line of best fit for study hours vs. test score is $\hat{y} = 5x + 55$. Predict the score for 8 hours of studying.

1
Identify what to substitute: we want to predict when $x = 8$ hours.
2
Substitute into the equation: $\hat{y} = 5(8) + 55 = 40 + 55 = 95$.
3
Interpret the slope: each additional hour of study is associated with 5 more points on the test.
A student who studies 8 hours is predicted to score 95.
Example 2 — Estimate a line from data

Data:

Hours (x)1234
Miles (y)35810

Estimate a line of best fit and use it to predict y at x = 5.

1
Estimate the slope using two points: from $(1, 3)$ to $(4, 10)$: $m \approx \dfrac{10-3}{4-1} = \dfrac{7}{3} \approx 2.3$.
2
Estimate the y-intercept using point $(1, 3)$: $3 = 2.3(1) + b \Rightarrow b \approx 0.7$.
3
Write the line: $\hat{y} \approx 2.3x + 0.7$.
4
Predict at $x = 5$: $\hat{y} \approx 2.3(5) + 0.7 = 11.5 + 0.7 = 12.2$ miles.
Predicted value at $x = 5$: approximately 12.2 miles.
Example 3 — Compute a residual

A line of best fit is $\hat{y} = 3x + 10$. A data point is $(4, 25)$. Find the residual.

1
Find the predicted value: $\hat{y} = 3(4) + 10 = 12 + 10 = 22$.
2
Identify the actual value: $y = 25$.
3
Compute the residual: actual $-$ predicted $= 25 - 22 = 3$.
Residual $= 3$. The point is 3 units above the line of best fit.

Practice Problems

A scatter plot shows that as x increases, y also increases. This is called:

Think about the direction: both variables move in the same direction (both up).
When both variables increase together, the relationship is called positive correlation. The scatter plot cloud slopes upward from left to right.

A line of best fit is $\hat{y} = 4x + 10$. Predict $y$ when $x = 6$.

Substitute $x = 6$ into the equation: $\hat{y} = 4(6) + 10$.
$\hat{y} = 4(6) + 10 = 24 + 10 = \mathbf{34}$.

The line of best fit is $\hat{y} = 2.5x + 30$. What does the slope 2.5 mean in context?

The slope of a line always describes the rate of change: how much y changes for each 1-unit increase in x.
The slope is the rate of change. A slope of 2.5 means for every 1-unit increase in x, y increases by 2.5. The y-intercept 30 is the starting value, not the slope.

For a data point where actual $y = 18$ and predicted $\hat{y} = 15$, what is the residual?

Residual = actual $-$ predicted. Subtract the predicted value from the actual value.
Residual $=$ actual $-$ predicted $= 18 - 15 = \mathbf{3}$. A positive residual means the point is above the line of best fit.

Predicting a y-value for an x that is far outside the range of the data is called:

Think about the prefix: "extra-" means outside. Predicting outside the data range is riskier because the trend may not continue.
Extrapolation means predicting beyond the observed data range. It is less reliable because the linear trend may not continue outside the measured data. Interpolation (predicting within the range) is more trustworthy.

A line of best fit for weekly cost ($) vs. number of orders is $\hat{y} = 3x + 20$. What does the y-intercept 20 represent?

The y-intercept is the value of y when $x = 0$. What does it mean for the cost when zero orders are placed?
When $x = 0$ (no orders), $\hat{y} = 3(0) + 20 = 20$. The y-intercept represents the fixed cost of $20 even when no orders are placed — like a base fee or overhead cost.

Using $\hat{y} = 6x + 2$, find the predicted value when $x = 3$.

Substitute $x = 3$: $\hat{y} = 6(3) + 2$.
$\hat{y} = 6(3) + 2 = 18 + 2 = \mathbf{20}$.

A line of best fit passes through $(1, 8)$ and $(3, 14)$. What is the slope?

Use the slope formula: $m = \dfrac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}$.
$m = \dfrac{14 - 8}{3 - 1} = \dfrac{6}{2} = \mathbf{3}$.

A negative residual means the actual data point is:

Residual = actual $-$ predicted. If residual is negative, actual is less than predicted. Is actual less than predicted when the point is above or below the line?
Residual $=$ actual $-$ predicted. A negative residual means actual $<$ predicted, so the data point lies below the line of best fit. A positive residual means the point is above the line.

A line of best fit has slope 3 and y-intercept 2. Predict $y$ when $x = 5$.

Write the equation first: $\hat{y} = 3x + 2$. Then substitute $x = 5$.
The equation is $\hat{y} = 3x + 2$. Substituting $x = 5$: $\hat{y} = 3(5) + 2 = 15 + 2 = \mathbf{17}$.
