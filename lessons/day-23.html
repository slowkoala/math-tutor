Day 23 Geometry

Transformations & Congruence

Key Vocabulary

Transformation
A rule that moves or changes every point of a figure. Types: translation, reflection, rotation, dilation.
Translation
A slide — every point moves the same distance in the same direction: \((x,y) \to (x+a, y+b)\).
Reflection
A flip over a line (line of reflection). Over the x-axis: \((x,y) \to (x,-y)\). Over y-axis: \((x,y) \to (-x,y)\).
Rotation
A turn about a center point. 90° CCW: \((x,y) \to (-y,x)\). 180°: \((x,y) \to (-x,-y)\).
Congruence
Two figures are congruent (\(\cong\)) if one can be obtained from the other by rigid motions (translations, reflections, rotations).
Pre-image / Image
The pre-image is the original figure; the image is the result after a transformation. Prime notation: \(A \to A'\).

Rigid Motions Preserve Congruence

Translations, reflections, and rotations are rigid motions — they preserve side lengths and angle measures. The image is always congruent to the pre-image.

Reflection over x-axis
\((x, y) \to (x, -y)\)
Reflection over y-axis
\((x, y) \to (-x, y)\)
Rotation 90° CCW
\((x, y) \to (-y, x)\)
Rotation 180°
\((x, y) \to (-x, -y)\)

Worked Examples

Triangle XYZ has vertices \(X(2,3)\), \(Y(4,1)\), \(Z(5,4)\). Translate by \((-3, 2)\). Find X'.

Rule: \((x,y) \to (x-3, y+2)\)

X: \((2-3, 3+2) = (-1, 5)\)

Y: \((4-3, 1+2) = (1, 3)\)

Z: \((5-3, 4+2) = (2, 6)\)

\(X' = (-1, 5)\)

Reflect point \(P(3, -5)\) over the y-axis.

Rule for reflection over y-axis: \((x,y) \to (-x, y)\)

\(P(3, -5) \to P'(-3, -5)\)

Rotate point \(Q(4, 2)\) by 90° counter-clockwise about the origin.

Rule for 90° CCW: \((x,y) \to (-y, x)\)

\(Q(4, 2) \to Q'(-2, 4)\)
