Day 23 Geometry

Transformations & Congruence

Vocabulary

Transformation
A rule that moves or changes every point of a figure. Types: translation, reflection, rotation, dilation.
Translation
A slide — every point moves the same distance in the same direction: $(x,y) \to (x+a,\ y+b)$.
Reflection
A flip over a line. Over the x-axis: $(x,y) \to (x,-y)$. Over the y-axis: $(x,y) \to (-x,y)$.
Rotation
A turn about a center point. 90° CCW: $(x,y) \to (-y,x)$. 180°: $(x,y) \to (-x,-y)$.
Congruence
Two figures are congruent ($\cong$) if one can be obtained from the other by rigid motions (translations, reflections, rotations).
Pre-image / Image
The pre-image is the original figure; the image is the result after a transformation. Prime notation: $A \to A'$.

Concept

Translations, reflections, and rotations are rigid motions — they preserve side lengths and angle measures. The image is always congruent to the pre-image.

A translation 4 units right: every point moves $(x,y) \to (x+4, y)$. The triangles are congruent.

Reflection over x-axis
$(x,y) \to (x,-y)$
Reflection over y-axis
$(x,y) \to (-x,y)$
Rotation 90° CCW
$(x,y) \to (-y,x)$
Rotation 180°
$(x,y) \to (-x,-y)$

Worked Examples

Example 1 — Apply a translation to all vertices

Triangle XYZ has vertices $X(2,3)$, $Y(4,1)$, $Z(5,4)$. Translate by $(-3, 2)$. Find the image vertices.

1
Write the rule: $(x,y) \to (x-3,\ y+2)$
2
$X(2,3) \to (2-3,\ 3+2) = (-1,\ 5)$
3
$Y(4,1) \to (4-3,\ 1+2) = (1,\ 3)$
4
$Z(5,4) \to (5-3,\ 4+2) = (2,\ 6)$
$X'(-1,5)$,  $Y'(1,3)$,  $Z'(2,6)$
Example 2 — Reflect a point over the y-axis

Reflect point $P(3,-5)$ over the y-axis.

1
Rule for reflection over y-axis: $(x,y) \to (-x,\ y)$
2
Negate the x-coordinate, keep y: $P(3,-5) \to P'(-3,-5)$
$P'(-3,\ -5)$
Example 3 — Rotate a point 90° counter-clockwise

Rotate point $Q(4,2)$ by 90° counter-clockwise about the origin.

1
Rule for 90° CCW: $(x,y) \to (-y,\ x)$
2
New x $= -y = -2$, new y $= x = 4$: $Q(4,2) \to Q'(-2,4)$
$Q'(-2,\ 4)$

Practice Problems

Point $A(3,-1)$ is translated by $(+2,+5)$. What is the x-coordinate of $A'$?

Add the translation value to the x-coordinate: $3 + 2 = ?$
$(3+2,\ -1+5) = (5,\ 4)$, so $x' = 5$.

Using the same translation $(+2,+5)$, what is the y-coordinate of $A'$?

Add 5 to the y-coordinate of A: $-1 + 5 = ?$
$y' = -1 + 5 = 4$.

Reflect point $(2,5)$ over the x-axis. What is the image?

Over the x-axis: $(x,y) \to (x,-y)$ — negate the y-coordinate only.
$(x,y) \to (x,-y)$, so $(2,5) \to (2,-5)$. The x-coordinate stays the same.

Reflect point $(-3,4)$ over the y-axis. What is the image?

Over the y-axis: $(x,y) \to (-x,y)$ — negate the x-coordinate only.
$(-x, y)$: negating $-3$ gives $+3$, so $(-3,4) \to (3,4)$.

Rotate $(5,2)$ by 90° counter-clockwise about the origin.

90° CCW rule: $(x,y) \to (-y,\ x)$. The new x-coordinate is $-y$.
$(x,y) \to (-y,x)$, so $(5,2) \to (-2,5)$.

Rotate point $(3,-1)$ by 180° about the origin.

180° rule: $(x,y) \to (-x,-y)$ — negate both coordinates.
$(3,-1) \to (-3,1)$: negate both. $-(-1) = 1$.

Which property is preserved by all rigid motions?

"Rigid" means the shape doesn't stretch or shrink. What can't change if nothing stretches?
Rigid motions preserve distances (side lengths) and angle measures — the image is always congruent to the pre-image. Coordinates change; reflections reverse orientation.

A translation maps $(1,4)$ to $(4,1)$. The rule is $(x+a,\ y+b)$. What is $a+b$?

Find $a$: $1+a=4$. Find $b$: $4+b=1$. Then add.
$a=3$ and $b=-3$, so $a+b=0$.

After reflecting a triangle over the y-axis, the image is:

Reflections are rigid motions. What do all rigid motions produce?
Reflections are rigid motions — they preserve all distances and angles, so the image is always congruent to the pre-image.

Which transformation reverses the orientation (handedness) of a figure?

Think of a mirror — it flips left and right. Which transformation works like a mirror?
A reflection flips orientation (like a mirror image). Translations and rotations preserve orientation; only reflections reverse it.
