Day 24 Geometry

Similarity & Dilations

Vocabulary

Similarity
Two figures are similar ($\sim$) if one can be obtained from the other by rigid motions and dilations. Same shape, possibly different size.
Dilation
A transformation that scales a figure by scale factor $k$ about a center point: $(x,y) \to (kx,\ ky)$.
Scale Factor
The ratio of corresponding lengths in similar figures: $k = \dfrac{\text{image length}}{\text{pre-image length}}$.
Corresponding Parts
Matching sides and angles in similar figures. Corresponding angles are equal; corresponding sides are proportional.
Proportion
An equation stating two ratios are equal: $\dfrac{a}{b} = \dfrac{c}{d}$. Used to find missing lengths in similar figures.
AA Similarity
If two angles of one triangle equal two angles of another, the triangles are similar (Angle-Angle criterion).

Concept

A dilation with scale factor $k$ centered at the origin multiplies every coordinate by $k$:

$(x,y) \xrightarrow{\times k} (kx,\ ky)$

Dilation by $k=2$: the green triangle is twice the size of the blue, with corresponding angles equal.

💡
Finding a missing side: Set up a proportion: $\dfrac{\text{side in } \triangle 1}{\text{corresponding side in } \triangle 2} = \dfrac{\text{other known side 1}}{\text{other known side 2}}$. Cross-multiply to solve.

Worked Examples

Example 1 — Find a missing side using similar triangles

Triangle ABC ~ Triangle DEF. $AB = 6$, $BC = 9$, and $DE = 4$. Find $EF$.

1
Set up a proportion using corresponding sides: $\dfrac{AB}{DE} = \dfrac{BC}{EF}$
2
Substitute: $\dfrac{6}{4} = \dfrac{9}{EF}$
3
Cross-multiply: $6 \cdot EF = 4 \cdot 9 = 36$
4
Divide: $EF = 36 \div 6 = 6$
$EF = 6$
Example 2 — Dilate a point by a fractional scale factor

A figure is dilated by scale factor $\tfrac{1}{3}$ from the origin. Point $P(9,-6)$ maps to $P'$. Find $P'$.

1
Rule: $(x,y) \to \left(\tfrac{1}{3}x,\ \tfrac{1}{3}y\right)$
2
$P(9,-6) \to P'\!\left(\tfrac{9}{3},\ \tfrac{-6}{3}\right) = P'(3,-2)$
3
Scale factor $k = \tfrac{1}{3} < 1$ → the image is smaller (a reduction).
$P'(3,\ -2)$ — a reduction
Example 3 — Find a missing side using scale factor

Two similar triangles have sides 5, 7, 9 and 10, 14, ?. Find the missing side.

1
Find the scale factor: $k = \dfrac{10}{5} = 2$   (confirm: $\dfrac{14}{7} = 2$ ✓)
2
Multiply the third side by $k$: $9 \times 2 = 18$
Missing side $= 18$

Practice Problems

Point $(4,6)$ is dilated from the origin by scale factor 3. What is the x-coordinate of the image?

Multiply the x-coordinate by the scale factor: $4 \times 3 = ?$
$(x,y) \to (3x, 3y)$: $4 \times 3 = 12$.

What is the y-coordinate of the image of $(4,6)$ after dilation by scale factor 3?

Multiply the y-coordinate by 3: $6 \times 3 = ?$
$6 \times 3 = 18$.

A dilation with scale factor $k = 2.5$ produces:

If $k > 1$ the image is larger; if $k < 1$ the image is smaller; if $k = 1$ it's the same size.
$k = 2.5 > 1$, so every side gets multiplied by 2.5 → the image is an enlargement.

△ABC ~ △XYZ with $AB = 8$ and $XY = 12$. What is the scale factor from △ABC to △XYZ?

$k = \dfrac{\text{image side}}{\text{pre-image side}} = \dfrac{XY}{AB}$
$k = \dfrac{12}{8} = 1.5$

Two similar triangles have sides 3, 4, 5 and 9, 12, ?. Find the missing side.

Find the scale factor from the known pairs: $9 \div 3 = ?$. Then multiply the third side.
$k = 9 \div 3 = 3$. Missing side $= 5 \times 3 = 15$.

In similar figures, which measurements are preserved?

Similar means same shape but possibly different size. Shape is determined by angles; size changes proportionally.
Similar figures have equal corresponding angles and proportional (not equal) corresponding sides. Perimeter and area scale by $k$ and $k^2$ respectively, so they are not preserved.

A triangle with perimeter 20 is dilated by scale factor $\tfrac{1}{2}$. What is the perimeter of the image?

Perimeter scales by the same factor as the sides.
$20 \times \tfrac{1}{2} = 10$.

Two triangles each have angles of 40° and 70°. Are they similar?

The AA criterion: if two pairs of angles match, the triangles are similar. How many angle pairs match here?
Both triangles have 40° and 70° — the third angle must also match (180° − 40° − 70° = 70°). Two matching angle pairs → similar by AA.

△PQR ~ △STU with $PQ = 5$, $ST = 15$, and $PR = 4$. Find $SU$.

Set up: $\dfrac{PQ}{ST} = \dfrac{PR}{SU}$, then cross-multiply.
$\dfrac{5}{15} = \dfrac{4}{SU} \Rightarrow 5 \cdot SU = 60 \Rightarrow SU = 12$.

A square with area 9 is dilated by scale factor 2. What is the area of the image?

Area scales by $k^2$. If $k = 2$, the area is multiplied by $2^2 = 4$.
$9 \times 2^2 = 9 \times 4 = 36$.
