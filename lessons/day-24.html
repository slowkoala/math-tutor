Day 24 Geometry

Similarity & Dilations

Key Vocabulary

Similarity
Two figures are similar (\(\sim\)) if one can be obtained from the other by a sequence of rigid motions and dilations. Same shape, possibly different size.
Dilation
A transformation that scales a figure by a scale factor \(k\) about a center point: \((x,y) \to (kx, ky)\).
Scale Factor
The ratio of corresponding lengths in similar figures: \(k = \dfrac{\text{image length}}{\text{pre-image length}}\).
Corresponding Parts
Matching sides and angles in similar figures. Corresponding angles are equal; corresponding sides are proportional.
Proportion
An equation stating that two ratios are equal: \(\dfrac{a}{b} = \dfrac{c}{d}\). Used to find missing lengths in similar figures.
AA Similarity
If two angles of one triangle equal two angles of another, the triangles are similar (Angle-Angle criterion).

Dilations & Scale Factor

A dilation with scale factor \(k\) centered at the origin multiplies every coordinate by \(k\):

\((x, y) \xrightarrow{\times k} (kx, ky)\)

Finding a missing side: Set up a proportion: \(\dfrac{\text{side in △1}}{\text{corresponding side in △2}} = \dfrac{\text{known side 1}}{\text{known side 2}}\). Cross-multiply to solve.

Worked Examples

Triangle ABC ~ Triangle DEF. AB = 6, BC = 9, and DE = 4. Find EF.

Set up proportion: \(\dfrac{AB}{DE} = \dfrac{BC}{EF}\)

\(\dfrac{6}{4} = \dfrac{9}{EF}\)

Cross-multiply: \(6 \cdot EF = 4 \cdot 9 = 36\)

\(EF = 6\)

A figure is dilated by scale factor \(\tfrac{1}{3}\) from the origin. Point \(P(9, -6)\) maps to \(P'\). Find \(P'\).

Rule: \((x,y) \to \left(\tfrac{1}{3}x, \tfrac{1}{3}y\right)\)

\(P(9, -6) \to P'\left(3, -2\right)\)

Scale factor \(< 1\) → the image is smaller (reduction).

Two similar triangles have sides 5, 7, 9 and 10, 14, ?. Find the missing side.

Find scale factor: \(k = \dfrac{10}{5} = 2\) (confirmed: \(\dfrac{14}{7} = 2\) ✓)

Missing side: \(9 \times 2 = 18\)
