Day 26 Geometry

Angles & Volume

Key Vocabulary

Supplementary Angles
Two angles that sum to 180°. If one is \(x\), the other is \(180° - x\).
Complementary Angles
Two angles that sum to 90°. If one is \(x\), the other is \(90° - x\).
Vertical Angles
Opposite angles formed by two intersecting lines. Vertical angles are always equal.
Parallel Lines & Transversal
A transversal crossing parallel lines creates: alternate interior angles (equal), co-interior angles (supplementary), corresponding angles (equal).
Volume of a Cylinder
\(V = \pi r^2 h\) — base area times height.
Volume of a Cone / Sphere
Cone: \(V = \tfrac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h\). Sphere: \(V = \tfrac{4}{3}\pi r^3\).

Angle Relationships & 3D Volume

Cylinder
\(V = \pi r^2 h\)
Cone
\(V = \dfrac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h\)
Sphere
\(V = \dfrac{4}{3}\pi r^3\)
Rectangular Prism
\(V = l \cdot w \cdot h\)

Worked Examples

Two angles are supplementary. One measures \(3x + 10\)° and the other \(x + 30\)°. Find x.

Supplementary means sum = 180°:

\((3x+10) + (x+30) = 180\)

\(4x + 40 = 180\)

\(4x = 140 \Rightarrow x = 35\)

Find the volume of a cylinder with radius 5 cm and height 12 cm. Leave answer in terms of \(\pi\).

Formula: \(V = \pi r^2 h\)

\(V = \pi (5)^2 (12) = \pi \cdot 25 \cdot 12 = 300\pi \text{ cm}^3\)

A cone has radius 6 and height 9. Find its volume. (Use \(\pi \approx 3.14\).)

Formula: \(V = \dfrac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h\)

\(V = \dfrac{1}{3}(3.14)(36)(9) = \dfrac{1}{3}(3.14)(324)\)

\(= \dfrac{1}{3}(1017.36) \approx 339.12 \text{ units}^3\)

Practice Problems

Two angles are complementary. One angle measures 38°. What is the other angle?
Two lines intersect. One angle formed is 73°. What is the measure of the vertical angle?
Two parallel lines are cut by a transversal. One corresponding angle measures 110°. What is the other corresponding angle?
Parallel lines are cut by a transversal. One alternate interior angle is 65°. The other alternate interior angle is:
  1. 65° — alternate interior angles are equal
  2. 115° — they are supplementary
  3. 25° — they are complementary
  4. Cannot be determined
