Day 26 Geometry

Angles & Volume

Vocabulary

Supplementary Angles
Two angles that sum to 180°. If one is $x$, the other is $180° - x$.
Complementary Angles
Two angles that sum to 90°. If one is $x$, the other is $90° - x$.
Vertical Angles
Opposite angles formed by two intersecting lines. Vertical angles are always equal.
Parallel Lines & Transversal
A transversal crossing parallel lines creates: alternate interior angles (equal), co-interior angles (supplementary), corresponding angles (equal).
Volume of a Cylinder
$V = \pi r^2 h$ — base area times height.
Volume of a Cone / Sphere
Cone: $V = \tfrac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h$. Sphere: $V = \tfrac{4}{3}\pi r^3$.

Concept

Parallel lines: corresponding angles are equal; co-interior angles are supplementary.

Cylinder
$V = \pi r^2 h$
Cone
$V = \dfrac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h$
Sphere
$V = \dfrac{4}{3}\pi r^3$
Rectangular Prism
$V = l \cdot w \cdot h$
💡
Memory trick for 3D volumes: Cylinder = $\pi r^2 h$. Cone = $\tfrac{1}{3}$ of a cylinder. Sphere = $\tfrac{4}{3}\pi r^3$.

Worked Examples

Example 1 — Supplementary angle equation

Two angles are supplementary. One measures $(3x + 10)°$ and the other $(x + 30)°$. Find $x$.

1
Supplementary angles sum to 180°: $(3x+10) + (x+30) = 180$
2
Combine like terms: $4x + 40 = 180$
3
$4x = 140 \Rightarrow x = 35$
$x = 35$
Example 2 — Cylinder volume

Find the volume of a cylinder with radius 5 cm and height 12 cm. Leave your answer in terms of $\pi$.

1
Write the formula: $V = \pi r^2 h$
2
Substitute: $V = \pi (5)^2 (12) = \pi \cdot 25 \cdot 12 = 300\pi \text{ cm}^3$
$V = 300\pi \approx 942 \text{ cm}^3$
Example 3 — Cone volume

A cone has radius 6 and height 9. Find its volume. (Use $\pi \approx 3.14$.)

1
Write the formula: $V = \dfrac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h$
2
Substitute: $V = \dfrac{1}{3}(3.14)(6)^2(9) = \dfrac{1}{3}(3.14)(36)(9)$
3
$= \dfrac{1}{3}(1017.36) \approx 339.12 \text{ units}^3$
$V \approx 339.12 \text{ units}^3$

Practice Problems

Two angles are supplementary. One angle measures 115°. What is the other angle (in degrees)?

Supplementary angles sum to 180°. Subtract 115 from 180.
$180° - 115° = 65°$

Two angles are complementary. One angle measures 38°. What is the other angle?

Complementary angles sum to 90°. Subtract 38 from 90.
$90° - 38° = 52°$

Two lines intersect. One angle formed is 73°. What is the measure of the vertical angle?

Vertical angles are always equal to each other.
Vertical angles are equal, so the other angle is also 73°.

Two parallel lines are cut by a transversal. One corresponding angle measures 110°. What is the other corresponding angle?

Corresponding angles are equal when the lines are parallel.
Corresponding angles are equal, so the other is also 110°.

Parallel lines are cut by a transversal. One alternate interior angle is 65°. The other alternate interior angle is:

Alternate interior angles are on opposite sides of the transversal, between the parallel lines. What's their relationship?
When lines are parallel, alternate interior angles are always equal.

Find the volume of a cylinder with radius 3 and height 7. Express your answer as $k\pi$ and enter the value of $k$.

Cylinder: $V = \pi r^2 h = \pi(3)^2(7) = \pi \cdot 9 \cdot 7$
$V = \pi(9)(7) = 63\pi$, so $k = 63$.

Find the volume of a sphere with radius 3. Express as $k\pi$ and enter $k$.

Sphere: $V = \tfrac{4}{3}\pi r^3 = \tfrac{4}{3}\pi(3)^3 = \tfrac{4}{3}\pi(27)$
$V = \tfrac{4}{3}\pi(27) = \tfrac{108}{3}\pi = 36\pi$, so $k = 36$.

Find the volume of a cone with radius 6 and height 4. Express as $k\pi$ and enter $k$.

Cone: $V = \tfrac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h = \tfrac{1}{3}\pi(6)^2(4) = \tfrac{1}{3}\pi(36)(4)$
$V = \tfrac{1}{3}\pi(144) = 48\pi$, so $k = 48$.

Find the volume of a rectangular prism with length 4, width 5, and height 6.

Rectangular prism: $V = l \cdot w \cdot h = 4 \cdot 5 \cdot 6$
$V = 4 \times 5 \times 6 = 120$

Angles $(2x + 15)°$ and $(3x - 5)°$ are supplementary. Find $x$.

$(2x+15) + (3x-5) = 180$. Combine like terms on the left side.
$5x + 10 = 180 \Rightarrow 5x = 170 \Rightarrow x = 34$
