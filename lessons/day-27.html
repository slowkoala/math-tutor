Practice Problems

Q1. How is $\sqrt{7}$ best classified?

Can $\sqrt{7}$ be written as a fraction $\tfrac{p}{q}$? What does its decimal expansion look like?
$\sqrt{7} \approx 2.6457\ldots$ — a non-terminating, non-repeating decimal. That means it is irrational.

Q2. Simplify $\sqrt{48}$.

Factor out the largest perfect square: $48 = 16 \times 3$
$\sqrt{48} = \sqrt{16 \cdot 3} = \sqrt{16}\cdot\sqrt{3} = 4\sqrt{3}$

Q3. Find the slope of the line through $(2, 5)$ and $(6, 13)$.

$m = \dfrac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1} = \dfrac{13-5}{6-2}$
$m = \dfrac{8}{4} = 2$

Q4. A line has slope $-3$ and passes through $(1, 4)$. What is the y-intercept?

Point-slope form: $y - 4 = -3(x - 1)$. Expand, then find $b$ when $x = 0$.
$y = -3x + 3 + 4 = -3x + 7$. The y-intercept is 7.

Q5. Solve: $2x - 5 > 9$

Add 5 to both sides, then divide by 2.
$2x > 14 \Rightarrow x > 7$

Q6. Is $(1, 1)$ a solution to $y > 2x - 1$?

Substitute $(1,1)$: Is $1 > 2(1) - 1$? Compute the right side.
$2(1)-1 = 1$, so we need $1 > 1$. This is false — the strict inequality $>$ is not satisfied when both sides are equal.

Q7. Solve the system $\begin{cases}y = 2x+1 \\ y = x+3\end{cases}$. What is the x-coordinate of the solution?

Set the two expressions equal: $2x + 1 = x + 3$
$2x+1 = x+3 \Rightarrow x = 2$. Then $y = 2(2)+1 = 5$. Solution: $(2, 5)$.

Q8. Solve by elimination: $\begin{cases}3x + 2y = 12 \\ 3x - 2y = 0\end{cases}$. What is the value of $y$?

Add the two equations together to eliminate $y$. What do you get?
Adding: $6x = 12 \Rightarrow x = 2$. Substituting back: $3(2) + 2y = 12 \Rightarrow 2y = 6 \Rightarrow y = 3$.

Q9. If $f(x) = x^2 - 3x + 1$, find $f(4)$.

Substitute $x = 4$: $f(4) = 4^2 - 3(4) + 1$
$f(4) = 16 - 12 + 1 = 5$

Q10. What is the initial value of $f(x) = 200 \cdot (0.75)^x$?

The initial value is $f(0)$. What is $(0.75)^0$?
$f(0) = 200 \cdot (0.75)^0 = 200 \cdot 1 = 200$

Q11. Find the average rate of change of $f(x) = 3x - 2$ from $x = 1$ to $x = 5$.

Rate of change $= \dfrac{f(5) - f(1)}{5 - 1}$. Compute $f(5)$ and $f(1)$.
$f(1) = 1$, $f(5) = 13$. Rate $= \dfrac{13-1}{4} = 3$. (Linear functions always have constant slope!)

Q12. Expand $(x+5)(x-3)$. What is the coefficient of the $x$ term?

FOIL: $(x+5)(x-3) = x^2 - 3x + 5x - 15$
$(x+5)(x-3) = x^2 + 2x - 15$. The coefficient of $x$ is 2.

Q13. Factor: $x^2 - x - 12$

Find two numbers that multiply to $-12$ and add to $-1$.
$-4 \times 3 = -12$ and $-4 + 3 = -1$ ✓. So $x^2 - x - 12 = (x-4)(x+3)$.

Q14. Find the y-coordinate of the vertex of $f(x) = x^2 + 6x + 5$.

Axis of symmetry: $x = -\dfrac{b}{2a} = -\dfrac{6}{2} = -3$. Now find $f(-3)$.
$f(-3) = (-3)^2 + 6(-3) + 5 = 9 - 18 + 5 = -4$. Vertex: $(-3, -4)$.

Q15. A line of best fit is $\hat{y} = 3x + 7$. Predict $y$ when $x = 5$.

Substitute $x = 5$ into $\hat{y} = 3x + 7$.
$\hat{y} = 3(5) + 7 = 15 + 7 = 22$

Q16. A scatter plot shows that as x increases, y generally decreases. This relationship is:

When y decreases as x increases, the slope of the trend line would be negative.
When y decreases as x increases, the data shows negative correlation.

Q17. Find the hypotenuse of a right triangle with legs 9 and 40.

$a^2 + b^2 = c^2$: compute $9^2 + 40^2$.
$81 + 1600 = 1681 = 41^2$. Hypotenuse $= 41$.  (9-40-41 is a Pythagorean triple.)

Q18. Reflect point $(-4, 7)$ over the x-axis. What is the y-coordinate of the image?

Reflecting over the x-axis negates the y-coordinate: $(x, y) \to (x, -y)$.
$(-4, 7) \to (-4, -7)$. The y-coordinate of the image is $-7$.

Q19. Two similar triangles have a scale factor of 3. The smaller triangle has perimeter 14. What is the perimeter of the larger triangle?

For similar figures, all lengths (including perimeter) scale by the same factor.
Scale factor 3 means all lengths are multiplied by 3. Larger perimeter $= 3 \times 14 = 42$.

Q20. Find the volume of a sphere with radius 6. Express as $k\pi$ and enter the value of $k$.

Sphere: $V = \tfrac{4}{3}\pi r^3$ with $r = 6$. Compute $6^3 = 216$, then $\tfrac{4}{3}(216)$.
$V = \tfrac{4}{3}\pi(216) = \tfrac{864}{3}\pi = 288\pi$, so $k = 288$.
